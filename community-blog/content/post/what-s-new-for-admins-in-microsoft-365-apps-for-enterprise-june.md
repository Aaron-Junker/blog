We've rounded up the latest and greatest news on Microsoft 365 Apps for
enterprise and we\'re excited to share it all with you! In this month\'s
blog, we discuss the General Availability (GA) of the new Inventory for
Microsoft 365 Apps admin center, a new customer story from Microsoft
partner and customer Avanade, the availability of a new Microsoft 365
licensing option, tricks and tips for onboarding devices in the
Microsoft 365 Apps admin center, our latest Microsoft 365 Apps AMA
session, and new Office Deployment Insider videos to help you get the
most out of Microsoft 365 Apps capabilities. 

 

## **General Availability of Inventory on Microsoft 365 Apps admin center** {#toc-hId--434670063}

 

In a recent announcement, we shared the General Availability release of
a new experience designed to provide IT admins with instant answers
regarding the status of their organization\'s Microsoft 365 Apps in the
enterprise environment. With the new Inventory on Microsoft 365 Apps
admin center, IT pros can quickly identify potential issues,
determine how many devices are running the latest security updates, and
more. Check out [this blog](https://aka.ms/AACinventoryGA) written by
one of the Microsoft 365 Apps Rangers, Martin Nothnagel, to learn all
about the new capabilities!

Avanade, Microsoft Global Alliance SI Partner of the Year, recently

desk tickets for IT. Additionally, as an innovator in the Microsoft
ecosystem, Avanade keeps their employees up-to-date with the latest
features, which helps them build confidence and trust with their own
customers as they modernize their IT environments to ensure remote and
hybrid workers have the latest features to be productive. To learn
more, [read the full Avanade customer story
here](https://customers.microsoft.com/en-us/story/1381603368388526598-avanade-partner-professional-services-m365-apps).   

 

## **Microsoft 365 E3: Unattended License option availability ** {#toc-hId-245388307}

 

We're excited to announce the availability of the Microsoft 365 E3:
Unattended License! As a new license option for Office apps, the
Unattended License allows the usage of Office apps with service accounts
or other non-user accounts, which is common for automation process
execution and can operate unattended.  IT admins deploying new or
existing solutions that use this unattended license can choose to update
Office apps via the Semi-Annual Enterprise Channel or Monthly Enterprise
Channel. Check out this blog to learn more about [enabling automation
for Microsoft 365 Apps for
enterprise](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-365-blog/enabling-automation-with-microsoft-365-apps-for-enterprise/ba-p/2494756). 

 

## **Tips and tricks for onboarding devices to the new Microsoft 365 Apps admin center ** {#toc-hId--1562066156}

 

In [this
blog](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-365-apps-for/how-to-troubleshoot-onboarding-devices-to-the-new-apps-admin/m-p/2183203),
the Microsoft 365 Apps Rangers share troubleshooting tips and tricks for
what to do when devices don't appear in the Microsoft 365 Apps admin
center after the new Inventory feature has been enabled. They cover root
causes as well as steps to take to help identify and resolve the
issue. You asked, we answered!  

 

## **Microsoft 365 Apps AMA featuring the Office Rangers** {#toc-hId-925446677}

session](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-365-apps-ama/bd-p/Microsoft365AppsAMA),
such as which Microsoft 365 subscriptions include Microsoft 365 Apps for
enterprise and the Teams Live Events feature for Microsoft Teams, when
Outlook Spaces may be released via General Availability, and how to
manage updates via update channels in Microsoft 365 Apps.
