**This blog was originally published on February 10, 2020. **The future
of work is here, and it's more collaborative than ever before. With
everyone becoming more globally connected, collaboration has become
paramount today. While it's not always possible to collaborate in
person, working together to share ideas and information can help us
improve efficiency in our work.

 

You've asked for shared editing in Visio, so today, we are excited to
present the first look at one of the most productivity-enhancing
features in Visio for the web: Real-time coauthoring.

 

## Better together! {#toc-hId--1410830024}

With the introduction of coauthoring, you and your collaborators with a
[Visio Plan 1](https://products.office.com/visio/visio-plan-1) or [Visio
Plan 2](https://products.office.com/visio/visio-plan-2) subscription can
now co-create and edit diagrams simultaneously using Visio for the web.
See your coauthors' edits as they happen and stay updated as the ideas
evolve on canvas.

 

Additionally, a simple presence indicator allows you to see *where*
other coauthors are working in the canvas, helps you to avoid conflicts
while editing. You can also add or reply to comments in a coauthoring
session in real time. Don't have a Visio subscription? You can still
view and reply to comments or add a shape/page level comment.

 

## Try it out yourself! {#toc-hId-1076682809}

We've started rolling out these changes in Visio for the web.
Visit [visio.office.com](https://office.live.com/start/visio.aspx) to
start collaborating on your diagrams!

 

Create a Visio drawing and invite others to work on the document with
you. You can set permissions and send the document link via e-mail or
chat. Anyone with edit permissions and a [Visio Plan
1](https://products.office.com/visio/visio-plan-1) or [Visio Plan
2](https://products.office.com/visio/visio-plan-2) subscription can
coauthor on the file you shared.

 

[![Collaborate by sharing
v2.png](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/170050iFADEA159A05D8B08/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999 "Collaborate by sharing v2.png"){.lia-media-image

Work together on a drawing as soon as the other person opens the link
and chooses to "Edit in browser," and make changes as you normally
would---without interruption. There are no other requirements to start a
seamless coauthoring experience.

 

[![Co-author with Visio for the
web](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/170073i035AEC3EEEC761A6/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999 "Visio co-authoring demo v2.0_13.gif"){.lia-media-image

Brainstorm with your team directly on the canvas about the process you
are designing, collectively work on a Visio drawing shared in Microsoft
Teams, share feedback with your colleagues on the drawing they shared,
or work together with your clients to come up with designs on their
transformation journey.

 

For more information on real time coauthoring in Visio for the web,
visit
[support.office.com](https://support.office.com/en-us/article/work-together-on-visio-drawings-413c0b5a-0d52-4ace-af85-8b9bf115bbbf#OfficeVersion=Web).
Tell us what other collaboration scenarios you'd like to use Visio for
in the comments section below.

 

## What's next for Visio collaboration features? {#toc-hId--730771654}

Our goal is to make the way you work easier. This is yet another step in
the journey of making Visio a more collaborative app that you can use to
brew ideas and work seamlessly with others.

 

Over the next several months, we'll keep adding features to provide an
enriched collaborative experience in Visio for the web. With your
suggestions and our know-how, it\'ll become easier and quicker to create
Visio diagrams with your team.

 

## Feedback {#toc-hId-1756741179}

We'd love to hear from you! Send us a smile or frown directly from the
app and let us know what you think.

 

Follow us on [Twitter](https://twitter.com/msvisio) to stay current on
the latest releases. You can email us at <tellvisio@microsoft.com> for
more detailed questions, and don't forget to send us your ideas through
the [UserVoice](https://visio.uservoice.com/) site to help improve
Visio.

 

Stay tuned for more updates on collaboration!
