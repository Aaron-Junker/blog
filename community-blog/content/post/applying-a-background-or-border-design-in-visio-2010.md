**First published on MSDN on Sep, 18 2009**

Visio 2010 introduces a new way to apply a background or border and
title design to your diagrams. The new Backgrounds and Borders & Titles
galleries on the Design tab in the ribbon let you choose from a variety
of styles and apply them with one click.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43911i4BFA120757ACED6C/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image

**Backgrounds**

For several versions, Visio has provided a way to apply a background
design to diagrams using shapes that you drag out from the Backgrounds
stencil that opens with many of the templates. In Visio 2010, you can do
this by clicking on a preview thumbnail in the Backgrounds gallery.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43912i7EBD8CC1B5EE9268/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image

When you apply a background, Visio creates a background page (named
"VBackground-1", if it's the first one), drops the background shape on
it, and assigns it to the foreground page.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43913i450B0E6AC5D0F230/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image
role="button"
li-image-url="https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43913i450B0E6AC5D0F230?v=v2"

If you right-click on the preview thumbnail in the Backgrounds gallery,
you can choose to apply it to all the pages in the document or just the
current page.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43914i66E0F29FA4272637/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image

Once the background page is created, you can click on its page tab to
put additional items on it that you want to appear on all the foreground
pages it's assigned to, like your company name or logo.

The color of the background can be defined by a
[theme](http://blogs.msdn.com/visio/archive/2009/09/15/themes-and-live-preview-in-visio-2010.aspx)
. After a background is applied, additional themes with background
colors appear in the Themes gallery. When one of these themes is
applied, the background takes the color from the theme.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43915i162CDB9DF323EF7B/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image

You can also click on the Background Color command at the bottom of the
Backgrounds gallery to pick a color.

**Borders & Titles**

You can apply border and title designs to your pages in a way similar to
backgrounds, using the Borders & Titles gallery on the Design tab. As
with the Backgrounds gallery, a background page is created to hold the
border shape. And you can right-click on the gallery thumbnail to choose
to apply the border to all the pages in the document or just the current
page.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43917iEAE371085C22B40F/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image
role="button"
li-image-url="https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43917iEAE371085C22B40F?v=v2"
li-image-display-id="'43917iEAE371085C22B40F'" li-message-uid="'237411'"
li-messages-message-image="true" li-bindable="" tabindex="0"
li-bypass-lightbox-when-linked="true"
li-use-hover-links="false"}]{.lia-inline-image-display-wrapper
.lia-image-align-inline

You can edit the border's title by clicking on the background page tab,
selecting the border shape, and typing a title. This title will appear
on all the foreground pages that the background page is assigned to, so
it works best as a document title rather than a title for individual
pages.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43918iE22D4456D334F036/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image

If you don't want the border's footer (which usually includes a page
number) to appear at the bottom of the page, you can right-click on the
border shape on the background page and choose Hide Footer.
