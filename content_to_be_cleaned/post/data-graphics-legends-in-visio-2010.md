**First published on MSDN on Oct, 14 2009**

Many of you are familiar with the work we did in Visio 2007 to make it
easier to surface data that exists in your diagram in a visual form.
[Data
graphics](http://blogs.msdn.com/visio/archive/2009/10/13/data-graphics-in-visio-2010.aspx)
allow you to add refreshable data visualizations to the shapes in your
diagram. See our previous posts [Microsoft Office Visio 2007
Released](http://blogs.msdn.com/visio/archive/2006/12/01/microsoft-office-visio-2007-released.aspx)
and [Customizing Data
Graphics](http://blogs.msdn.com/visio/archive/2007/01/02/customizing-data-graphics.aspx)
for more information.

  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **No data**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    **Data graphics!**
  [[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43959i2D8079B65B9F6007/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image role="button" li-image-url="https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43959i2D8079B65B9F6007?v=v2" li-image-display-id="'43959i2D8079B65B9F6007'" li-message-uid="'237464'" li-messages-message-image="true" li-bindable="" tabindex="0" li-bypass-lightbox-when-linked="true" li-use-hover-links="false"}]{.lia-inline-image-display-wrapper .lia-image-align-inline style="width: 231px;"}](https://msdnshared.blob.core.windows.net/media/TNBlogsFS/BlogFileStorage/blogs_msdn/visio/WindowsLiveWriter/7cf57279c642_FD89/image_2.png)   [[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43960i76F959F15AA18BA5/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image role="button" li-image-url="https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43960i76F959F15AA18BA5?v=v2" li-image-display-id="'43960i76F959F15AA18BA5'" li-message-uid="'237464'" li-messages-message-image="true" li-bindable="" tabindex="0" li-bypass-lightbox-when-linked="true" li-use-hover-links="false"}]{.lia-inline-image-display-wrapper .lia-image-align-inline style="width: 231px;"}](https://msdnshared.blob.core.windows.net/media/TNBlogsFS/BlogFileStorage/blogs_msdn/visio/WindowsLiveWriter/7cf57279c642_FD89/image_4.png)
  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 

 

 

One thing users have asked us for is a way to describe the data graphics
used in the diagram -- a legend. Visio 2010 adds the ability to insert a
legend that documents the data bars, icon sets and color by values in
data graphics applied to shapes on the page. You can do this using the
Insert Legend button on the Data tab.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43961i07A6E79AA3680DDD/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image
role="button"
li-image-url="https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43961i07A6E79AA3680DDD?v=v2"
li-image-display-id="'43961i07A6E79AA3680DDD'" li-message-uid="'237464'"
li-messages-message-image="true" li-bindable="" tabindex="0"

li-use-hover-links="false"}]{.lia-inline-image-display-wrapper
.lia-image-align-inline

Visio creates the legend at the upper right corner of the page. The
legend contains a separate section for each data field referenced in the
data graphics' definitions. The descriptions for each legend item are
obtained from the data graphics.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43962i30B44DF2769D743F/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image
role="button"

The legend is customizable, so you can add, remove and rename sections
and shapes to make the legend look just right for your particular
diagram. Legends also pick up the
[theme](http://blogs.msdn.com/visio/archive/2009/09/15/themes-and-live-preview-in-visio-2010.aspx)
applied to the page, or they can be manually formatted.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43963i3CA8D4DA8A2F7E02/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image
role="button"
li-image-url="https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43963i3CA8D4DA8A2F7E02?v=v2"
li-image-display-id="'43963i3CA8D4DA8A2F7E02'" li-message-uid="'237464'"
li-messages-message-image="true" li-bindable="" tabindex="0"
li-bypass-lightbox-when-linked="true"
li-use-hover-links="false"}]{.lia-inline-image-display-wrapper
.lia-image-align-inline
style="width: 310px;"}](https://msdnshared.blob.core.windows.net/media/TNBlogsFS/BlogFileStorage/blogs_msdn/visio/WindowsLiveWriter/7cf57279c642_FD89/image_10.png)

 

The legend is made up of a number of shapes and uses
[containers](http://blogs.msdn.com/visio/archive/2009/08/25/organizing-diagrams-with-containers.aspx)
to keep the different parts of the legend organized. The top-most shape
is a list, a special type of container that arranges its members in a
regular, linear pattern. The members of that list are containers, each
of which represents a data field from the data graphics. Inside each
container is another list, this one invisible, which keeps the
individual legend items neatly arranged.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43964i4359BD3265AD0F66/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image
role="button"
li-image-url="https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43964i4359BD3265AD0F66?v=v2"
li-image-display-id="'43964i4359BD3265AD0F66'" li-message-uid="'237464'"
li-messages-message-image="true" li-bindable="" tabindex="0"
li-bypass-lightbox-when-linked="true"
li-use-hover-links="false"}]{.lia-inline-image-display-wrapper
.lia-image-align-inline
style="width: 298px;"}](https://msdnshared.blob.core.windows.net/media/TNBlogsFS/BlogFileStorage/blogs_msdn/visio/WindowsLiveWriter/7cf57279c642_FD89/image_12.png)

You can select a legend item and use the arrow keys on your keyboard to
reorder them, or you can drag them around the list. You can also drag
them out, delete them or drag your own shapes in. The same can be done
with the containers that correspond to each data field. If you click the
blue insert arrow on the outer list, Visio adds an empty container for
your own use. While this will not have the inner list, you are free to
add any shapes you wish.

[[![](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43965i503E7B9D890E3D78/image-size/large?v=v2&px=999){.lia-media-image
role="button"
li-image-url="https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/43965i503E7B9D890E3D78?v=v2"
li-image-display-id="'43965i503E7B9D890E3D78'" li-message-uid="'237464'"
li-messages-message-image="true" li-bindable="" tabindex="0"
li-bypass-lightbox-when-linked="true"
li-use-hover-links="false"}]{.lia-inline-image-display-wrapper
.lia-image-align-inline
style="width: 356px;"}](https://msdnshared.blob.core.windows.net/media/TNBlogsFS/BlogFileStorage/blogs_msdn/visio/WindowsLiveWriter/7cf57279c642_FD89/image_14.png)

To preserve the customizations you might make to the legend, Visio does
not update it as you edit your data graphics or change which data
graphics are applied to shapes on the page. Simply insert a new legend
once you are done making changes. Delete the existing one or move it out
of the way if you need to bring anything over.

Finally, for the developers out there, you can drop a legend using
Page.DropLegend in the Visio API and specify custom outer list and field
container masters.

We're happy to be able to close this gap in data visualization and are
interested in your feedback via [Send a
Smile](http://blogs.technet.com/office2010/archive/2009/07/15/tell-us-what-you-think-about-office-2010-technical-preview.aspx)
or a comment here on the blog.
